The Future of Audio Content

Published on December 24, 2024 · 5 min read

In an era where attention is the most valuable currency, audio content has emerged as a powerful medium for reaching audiences. Podcasts have exploded in popularity, and audiobooks now represent a significant portion of the publishing market. But what about the vast ocean of written content that exists on the web?

The Accessibility Challenge

Millions of articles, blog posts, and documentation pages are published daily. Yet many readers struggle to find time to sit down and read them. Commuters, busy professionals, and people with visual impairments all face barriers to consuming written content.

Text-to-speech technology has traditionally offered a solution, but the robotic, unnatural voices made long-form listening unbearable. This is where neural TTS systems like Kokoro have changed the game, offering voices that are nearly indistinguishable from human speakers.

How SonoTxt Works

Looking Forward

As AI voice technology continues to improve, we expect audio versions of written content to become the norm rather than the exception. The future is multimodal, and SonoTxt is here to help you get there.
